Data Collection and Cleaning

This is the first and most important step. A data analyst must know how to get data from different sources (like databases or spreadsheets) and then clean it up. This involves fixing errors, removing duplicate entries, and organizing the data so it's ready for analysis.

Data Analysis and Interpretation

This is the core of the job. A data analyst uses statistical methods and analytical techniques to find trends, patterns, and insights within the data. They ask questions like:

  • Which product is our best-seller?
  • Which customers are most likely to leave?
  • How do sales in one region compare to another?

    • Data Visualization and Reporting

    After finding insights, a data analyst must present them in a way that is easy for everyone to understand. They build interactive dashboards and reports using tools like Power BI. This helps business leaders see the results of the analysis at a glance and make quick decisions.

    Collaboration and Communication

    A data analyst doesn't work alone. They often work with different teams, such as marketing, sales, and finance, to understand their data needs. They need to be good communicators to explain their findings and recommendations to people who may not be technical experts.
